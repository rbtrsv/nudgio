THE FIVE QUESTIONS

Question Weight Action
1. Can the founders execute? 50% MUST PASS
2. Is the timing right? 20% MUST PASS
3. Can they acquire customers cheaply? 15% MUST PASS
4. Will customers stay? 10% Adjusts valuation
5. Can they defend their position? 5% Adjusts valuation
If any of questions 1-3 fail → REJECT immediately
V7 CAPITAL

1CAN THE FOUNDERS EXECUTE?

Weight: 50% | Type: Must Pass

Binary Checklist — Must Pass 7 of 9

Domain expertise: 5+ years in this industry
Technical competence: Can build product themselves (if tech)
Prior startup experience: Founded/C-level at startup before
Resilience signal: Overcame major setback
Speed: Shipped meaningful product in <6 months
Learning rate: Pivoted based on user feedback
Insight: Non-obvious insight about market
Commitment: Full-time, no side projects, vesting
Partnership: Complementary co-founders, 1+ year together
SCORE: ___/9 PASS: ≥7 | FAIL: ≤6 → REJECT

Red Flags (Auto-Reject)

  • Part-time founders at Seed+
  • Co-founders met <3 months ago
  • Serial wantrepreneur (5+ failed ideas, no learnings)
  • Blames others for past failures

2IS THE TIMING RIGHT?

Weight: 20% | Type: Must Pass

The "Why Now?" Test

Answer in ONE sentence: This works NOW because: _________________________________

Pass Criteria (Must Have ONE)

  • Technology shift: AI models, mobile internet, blockchain
  • Regulatory change: Fintech licensing, data privacy
  • Behavioral change: Remote work, creator economy
  • Cost structure change: Cloud infrastructure, API economy
  • Underserved segment: SMB software, emerging markets

Examples

Company Why Now
Stripe (2010) APIs now make payments programmable
Uber (2009) Smartphones + GPS + payments converge
Figma (2016) WebGL enables design tools in browser
If you cannot articulate "why now" → REJECT

3CAN THEY ACQUIRE CUSTOMERS CHEAPLY?

Weight: 15% | Type: Must Pass

Capital Efficiency Gates

Pre-Revenue ($0 ARR)

CAC estimate ≤ $500 (B2C) or ≤ $5K (B2B)
Organic channel exists (SEO, viral, community)
10 design partners committed (will pay at launch)

Post-Revenue ($1M+ ARR)

Metric Threshold Actual Pass/Fail
LTV/CAC ≥ 3.0x ____x ☐
CAC Payback ≤ 12 months ____ mo ☐
Magic Number ≥ 0.75 ____ ☐
Burn Multiple ≤ 2.0 ____ ☐
Magic Number = New ARR ÷ Sales & Marketing Spend Burn Multiple = Cash Burned ÷ New ARR Added
Fail any metric → 50% valuation discount OR reject

User-initiated referrals happening

Post-Revenue

Metric Threshold Actual Pass/Fail
Net Retention ≥ 100% ____% ☐
Gross Retention (Month 12) ≥ 85% ____% ☐
Cohort Behavior Expanding _____ ☐
If retention failing → -30% valuation OR reject

5CAN THEY DEFEND POSITION?

Weight: 5% | Type: Adjusts Valuation

Moat Assessment

Network effects (value increases with users)
High switching costs (data lock-in, integrations)
Proprietary data/IP (patents, unique dataset)
Brand/trust (regulated industry)
Scale economies (marketplace, infrastructure)
Has at least ONE moat: No discount ZERO moats: -20% valuation (Not a rejection criterion)

VALUATION FORMULAS

Seed ($0-$1M ARR)

Step 1: Regional median seed valuation Median: $______M Step 2: Founder adjustment • Passed 9/9 criteria: ×1.5 • Passed 7-8/9: ×1.0 • Passed 5-6/9: ×0.7 Step 3: Timing adjustment • Clear "why now": ×1.2 • Weak "why now": ×1.0 Intrinsic Value = Median × Founder × Timing

Series A/B ($1-$20M ARR)

P/S Multiple = (Growth% × 20) × Quality Multiplier Quality Multiplier: • Passed all Q3 gates: 1.3x • Passed 3 of 4: 1.0x • Passed 2 of 4: 0.7x Intrinsic Value = ARR × P/S Multiple

Growth ($20M+ ARR)

Rule of 40 = Growth% + EBITDA% P/S Multiple: • Score ≥60%: 15x • Score 50-60%: 12x • Score 40-50%: 9x • Score <40%: REJECT Moat adjustment: • 1+ moats: ×1.0 • 0 moats: ×0.8 Intrinsic Value = ARR × P/S × Moat

Buyout (>$100M Revenue)

Target: 2.5-3.0x MOIC, 20-25% IRR over 5 years Exit EBITDA (Y5) × Exit Multiple (10-12x) = Exit Value ÷ Target MOIC (2.5-3.0x) = Max Entry Price Intrinsic Value = Max Entry Price
